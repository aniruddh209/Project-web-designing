Iconic Monuments of India

India's architectural heritage spans millennia, from ancient temples to colonial-era buildings.

Taj Mahal

Built by Shah Jahan in memory of Mumtaz Mahal, this white marble mausoleum in Agra is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.

Qutub Minar

The tallest brick minaret in the world at 73 meters, built in 1193. The complex includes the Iron Pillar that has resisted corrosion for over 1600 years.

Hawa Mahal

The "Palace of Winds" in Jaipur features 953 small windows (jharokhas) decorated with intricate latticework, built in 1799 for royal women to observe street festivals.

Charminar

Built in 1591, this mosque and monument in Hyderabad is the iconic symbol of the city, featuring four grand arches facing the cardinal directions.

Did You Know?

The Floating Post Office

India has the world's largest postal network with over 150,000 post offices, including a unique floating post office on Dal Lake in Srinagar, Kashmir. Built in 2011, it operates from a beautifully crafted houseboat.

The post office not only provides regular services but also sells postage stamps and products that showcase Kashmiri culture and heritage.
